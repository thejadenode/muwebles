Who are we?
The Philippine Archipelago, composed of 7,641 islands, is home for a large variety of trees. As a tropical country, this is not a surprise. History can tell that we have utilized our trees with such skill, which is very evident by the different designs of bahay-kubos or what we call as Nipa Huts.

And it’s not just the exterior of our houses! We have also been using our local trees as materials in making furniture such as beds, tables, and chairs. Why wouldn’t we? It’s very durable and is abundant in our surroundings. It’s very durable that in the earlier times, it was even used for kalesas, which are mainly used for transportation! Imagine the stress and everyday load of those kalesas, and how strong the woods are to be able to withstand those.

We focused our business on showcasing our local tables and chairs made from our finest woods to preserve the culture that we had since before. Along the furniture are intricate design patterns made by our very own furniture makers which you’ll surely love.

With your efforts and support, you won’t just be acquiring such preciously natural and locally made tables and chairs, but you will also be helping the local furniture industry, as well as giving new jobs, and sustaining old ones.
